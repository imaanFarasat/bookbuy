What Is Nail Fungus? (Onychomycosis)

Nail fungus, medically known as onychomycosis, is a common fungal infection affecting fingernails or toenails. It thrives in warm, moist environments, causing discoloration, thickening, and crumbling of the nails. Onychomycosis can be unsightly and sometimes painful if left untreated.

Common Causes Of Nail Fungus

Nail fungus is often caused by dermatophytes, yeasts, and molds. These fungi thrive in damp environments like public showers, pools, and gyms. Walking barefoot in these areas increases your risk. Sweaty feet, tight shoes, and minor nail injuries also contribute to fungal growth.

Symptoms Of Nail Fungus To Watch For

Early signs include a white or yellow spot under the nail tip. As the infection progresses, the nail may thicken, discolor (yellow, brown, or green), become brittle, and separate from the nail bed. A foul odor may also accompany the infection.

kinds of nail fungus

Several types of fungi can cause nail infections, each presenting slightly different symptoms. Distal subungual onychomycosis is the most common, affecting the nail tip and underside. White superficial onychomycosis appears as white spots on the nail surface. Other types include proximal subungual onychomycosis, affecting the base of the nail, and candidal onychomycosis, commonly seen in people with weakened immune systems.

foot and nail fungus

Foot and nail fungus often occur together, especially in individuals with conditions like athlete's foot. The warm, moist environment of shoes creates ideal conditions for fungal growth. Keeping feet dry and practicing good hygiene can help prevent both conditions.

toe nail fungus

Toenail fungus is especially prevalent due to the dark, moist environment inside shoes. It's more common than fingernail fungus and can be more stubborn to treat. Early treatment is essential to prevent the infection from spreading and causing further damage.

  • More common than fingernail fungus
  • Dark, moist environment inside shoes promotes growth
  • Can be stubborn to treat
  • Early treatment is essential

fake nails and fungus

Artificial nails can increase the risk of fungal infections if moisture gets trapped between the artificial and natural nail. Proper application, removal, and nail hygiene are crucial to minimize this risk. Avoid prolonged use and allow your natural nails to breathe periodically.

Practicing good hygiene is key. Keep your nails trimmed and clean, and dry your feet thoroughly after showering. Avoid walking barefoot in public areas like gyms and pools. Wear breathable shoes and socks, and change them regularly. Treat athlete's foot promptly to prevent it from spreading to nails.

how to remove nail fungus

Completely removing nail fungus requires consistent treatment, which can take several months depending on the severity. Topical or oral antifungal medications, prescribed by a doctor, are the most effective methods. In severe cases, surgical removal of the infected nail may be necessary.

  • Requires consistent treatment
  • Can take several months
  • Topical or oral antifungal medications most effective
  • Surgical removal may be necessary in severe cases

What is the best treatment for nail fungus?

The best treatment depends on the severity and type of infection. Consult a doctor for personalized advice, as they may prescribe oral or topical antifungal medications or recommend laser treatment.

How long does it take to cure nail fungus?

Treatment can take several months, even up to a year, for the nail to grow back fully healthy. Consistency with the prescribed treatment is crucial.

Is nail fungus contagious?

Yes, nail fungus is contagious and can spread from person to person through direct contact or shared surfaces like shower floors and nail clippers.

Can nail fungus go away on its own?

It's unlikely that nail fungus will go away without treatment. It often worsens over time and may cause pain or discomfort.

How can I prevent nail fungus?

Practice good hygiene by keeping your nails trimmed, dry, and clean. Avoid walking barefoot in public areas and wear breathable shoes.

What are the symptoms of toenail fungus?

Symptoms include thickening, discoloration (yellow, brown, or green), brittleness, crumbling, and separation of the nail from the nail bed.

Is laser treatment effective for nail fungus?

Laser treatment can be effective but may require multiple sessions and is not guaranteed to work for everyone.

Can I use home remedies for nail fungus?

Home remedies like tea tree oil or vinegar soaks may help in mild cases, but consult a doctor for persistent or worsening infections.

Is nail fungus painful?

While not always painful, nail fungus can cause discomfort, especially as it thickens and puts pressure on the surrounding skin.

Can I wear nail polish with nail fungus?

It's best to avoid nail polish during treatment as it can trap moisture and worsen the infection. Allow your nails to breathe.

What causes nail fungus?

Nail fungus is caused by various fungi that thrive in warm, moist environments. Common culprits include dermatophytes, yeasts, and molds.

How do I know if I have nail fungus?

Look for discoloration, thickening, brittleness, and changes in nail shape. Consult a doctor for a proper diagnosis.

Can children get nail fungus?

Yes, children can get nail fungus, though it's less common than in adults. Similar preventive measures apply.

Are there over-the-counter treatments for nail fungus?

Yes, there are over-the-counter antifungal creams and lacquers, but they may not be as effective as prescription medications.

Does diabetes increase the risk of nail fungus?

Yes, people with diabetes are at higher risk of nail fungus and other foot problems due to impaired circulation and weakened immune systems.

Should I see a dermatologist or podiatrist for nail fungus?

Both dermatologists and podiatrists can diagnose and treat nail fungus. Choose based on your preference and insurance coverage.

Can I get nail fungus from a pedicure?

Yes, unsanitary pedicure tools can transmit nail fungus. Ensure the salon follows proper hygiene practices.

What is the difference between toenail fungus and fingernail fungus?

Toenail fungus is more common due to the dark, moist environment of shoes. Treatment approaches are similar.

Can I prevent nail fungus recurrence?

Following preventive measures like proper hygiene and avoiding exposure to damp environments can reduce the risk of recurrence.
