Shellac Nail Polish vs Gel

Shellac and gel nails both offer extended wear and a glossy finish, but they differ in formulation and application. Shellac is a hybrid polish, thinner than gel, and cured under UV/LED light. Gel is thicker and requires more filing for removal. Understanding the nuances of shellac and gel helps you choose the perfect long-lasting manicure for your lifestyle. Consider factors like application time, removal process, and overall nail health when making your decision.

Durability and Longevity Comparison

Shellac typically lasts around two weeks without chipping, while gel nails can extend up to three weeks. Factors like nail care and lifestyle impact longevity for both.

While gel often boasts slightly longer wear, shellac’s thinner formula can feel more natural and less prone to lifting.

  • Shellac: Average lifespan of 2 weeks.
  • Gel: Average lifespan of 3 weeks.
  • Both can be affected by lifestyle and nail care routines.

Application Process Differences

Shellac application mirrors traditional polish with thin coats cured under UV/LED light. Gel application involves thicker coats and more precise shaping before curing.

The thinner coats of shellac make it easier to apply evenly, while the thicker viscosity of gel requires more practice for flawless application.

  • Shellac applies like regular nail polish, but requires curing.
  • Gel application involves thicker layers and more shaping.

Step-by-Step Removal Guide

Start by filing the top layer of the shellac to break the seal. Then, soak acetone-saturated cotton balls on each nail and wrap them tightly with foil. After 10-15 minutes, gently push off the softened polish.

After removing the shellac, hydrate your nails with cuticle oil to replenish moisture and maintain nail health.

  • File the top layer of the shellac.
  • Soak cotton pads in acetone and place on nails.
  • Wrap nails in foil.
  • Wait 10-15 minutes.
  • Gently push off the shellac.

Tools and Materials Needed for At-Home Removal

Gather 100% acetone, cotton balls or pads, aluminum foil, an orangewood stick, a nail file, and cuticle oil for a successful at-home shellac removal.

Using the right tools ensures a safe and efficient removal process, minimizing the risk of damage to your natural nails.

  • 100% acetone
  • Cotton balls or pads
  • Aluminum foil
  • Orangewood stick or cuticle pusher
  • Nail file
  • Cuticle oil

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Removing Shellac at Home

Avoid peeling or picking off shellac, as this can damage your natural nails. Use pure acetone and ensure proper soaking time for effective removal.

Peeling or picking off shellac can thin and weaken the nail, leading to breakage and peeling. Patience and proper removal techniques are crucial.

  • Peeling off the shellac.
  • Using acetone with a low concentration.
  • Not soaking the nails long enough.
  • Scraping the nails too aggressively.

What is shellac nail polish?

Shellac is a hybrid nail polish that combines the ease of application of regular polish with the durability and long-lasting wear of gel. It requires curing under a UV/LED lamp.

How long does shellac nail polish last?

Shellac typically lasts for around two weeks without chipping or fading.

Is shellac the same as gel nail polish?

While both offer extended wear and shine, shellac is a thinner, soak-off gel polish, whereas traditional gel requires filing for removal.

Can I remove shellac nail polish at home?

Yes, you can safely remove shellac at home using acetone, cotton balls/pads, and foil.

CND Shellac is known for its high-quality formula, long-lasting wear, high-gloss shine, and easy removal process.
